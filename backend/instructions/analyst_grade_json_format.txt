üö® PDF-FRIENDLY EXPLANATION REQUIREMENTS (CRITICAL):
==========================================

ALL explanations MUST be detailed, complete, and PDF-ready:

‚úÖ **LENGTH**: Minimum 2-4 sentences per explanation (3-5 for complex topics)
‚úÖ **COMPLETE SENTENCES**: Full grammatical sentences, not fragments
‚úÖ **CAUSAL REASONING**: Use "because", "therefore", "which means", "as a result"
‚úÖ **CONTEXT-RICH**: Assume reader has no prior knowledge of the company
‚úÖ **INVESTOR-FOCUSED**: Always tie back to investment implications
‚úÖ **SPECIFIC**: Reference actual data, market conditions, not generic statements
‚úÖ **CONNECTIVE**: Show how one factor affects another (chains of reasoning)

EXPLANATION QUALITY EXAMPLES:
==========================================

‚ùå BAD (too brief, no context):
"explanation": "This shows market share decline."

‚ùå BAD (generic, no specifics):
"explanation": "The company faces challenges in a competitive market which may impact future performance."

‚úÖ GOOD (detailed, causal, investor-focused):
"explanation": "The sustained market share erosion from 24% to 19% over the past eight quarters reflects structural competitive disadvantages rather than cyclical factors. This decline is driven by two interconnected issues: First, the company's delayed 4G rollout in tier-1 cities allowed competitors to capture high-ARPU customers who prioritize network quality over price. Second, the company's elevated debt burden constrains capital allocation to network upgrades, creating a negative feedback loop where service quality deteriorates relative to peers, which accelerates premium subscriber churn. From an investment perspective, this market share loss directly translates to revenue pressure and reduced operating leverage, making it increasingly difficult to generate the cash flows needed to service debt obligations. The trajectory suggests that without external intervention‚Äîeither through capital infusion or industry-wide tariff increases‚Äîthe company's ability to compete for high-value subscribers will continue to weaken, potentially leading to a permanent relegation to lower-margin customer segments."

‚úÖ GOOD (connects financial metrics with implications):
"explanation": "The EBITDA margin compression from 32% to 26% is not merely a profitability concern‚Äîit represents a fundamental deterioration in the unit economics of the business. This margin pressure stems from three simultaneous factors extracted from the quarterly reports: network costs increasing due to deferred maintenance catching up, spectrum amortization expenses remaining fixed while revenues decline, and competitive pricing preventing cost pass-through to customers. For equity investors, margins below 28% are critical because at that threshold, the company's operating cash flow barely covers interest expenses, leaving insufficient funds for network capex. This creates strategic inflexibility where management must choose between maintaining service quality (and burning cash) or cutting costs (and losing more subscribers). The margin trajectory therefore serves as a real-time indicator of the company's viability as a going concern, making it arguably more important than top-line revenue figures for assessing investment risk."
